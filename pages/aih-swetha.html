Artists in Highlights
Swetha Kanithi
This week we cover on @swethakanithi.illustrator our weekly artist in highlight series.The aim of the series is to showcase homegrown design talents and help inspire others..
Q1. - What inspires you?
I remember reading Enid Blyton as a kid and thinking how beautiful and detailed the drawings were, almost as if you were in the scenes that were illustrated. Children's books are my biggest inspiration and a few writers like Neil Gaiman and Elif Shafaq who describe their fictional worlds with such details! I am also drawn to nature a lot and love looking at classic botanical drawings. Also, as crazy as it sounds, I collect leaves from different parts of the world I travel to. I love world cinema that ranges from Satyajit Ray to Wes Anderson and Kurowasa. And of course, food is a big part of my life and always finds its way into my work.
Q2. - What is your creative process like?
Every time something inspires me to draw, I click a lot of pictures on my phone of real-life scenarios. I then take them to my drawing desk and draw various options for compositions and figure out what I want to work on for my final piece. I also collect a lot of reference photos of things I like on the internet and when I want to draw, I go back to those visuals to remind myself of the things I like to bring into my Illustrations.
Q3. - What advice would you give to a younger self?
Always keep a sketchbook with you.
After diving deeper you quickly realise that the graphic design community embedded within the lofi culture is rich and diverse. But there are some themes that you encounter again and again. Nostalgia and a love of setting retro images within modern settings or placed in a interstellar background are some of the common themes that immediately grab your attention. The music goes seamlessly with theimages and has a timeless feel to it, same as the art. The collages highlighted above are incredible in their own right but there work in one way or another has been used in the lofi culture
Q4. - What are the themes you gravitate towards?
I gravitate towards themes like nature, culture, food, humour, pop culture, architecture and abstract.

About
Pixelate is a design community-based in Vellore, with an aim to bring design enthusiasts and creatives in VIT under one roof. Our main objective is to internally improve the design standards of our college by providing designers to those who need em. We as a community help new designers understand the design principles, techniques that can help them down the line improve their own designs, and most importantly we are dedicated to creating designs that stand out.
